PROJECT NOTES

NEUE HAUS was the foundry's first commission — a neo-grotesque system designed for a municipal identity program in Lower Austria. The brief demanded neutrality with character. We delivered 9 weights across 3 widths, 847 glyphs each, full Latin Extended-A/B, Cyrillic, and Greek. The client chose a competitor's geometric sans two weeks before delivery.

STABIL emerged from a requirement for tabular financial reporting across 12 languages. Monospaced, 7 weights, lining and old-style figures, slashed zero, alternate currency glyphs. The banking consortium merged. The project was shelved.

METHODOLOGY

PROCESS

01 / RESEARCH

Historical precedent analysis. Archive visits. Competitive landscaping. Language support mapping. Technical constraint definition. No sketches until the brief is absolute.

02 / DRAWING

Control characters first: H, O, n, o, p, v. Then the system. Every weight drawn as a master. No interpolation until the extremes are resolved. Spacing developed in parallel.

03 / ENGINEERING

Kerning by class. Feature code for every OpenType requirement. Hinting for target environments. Variable font masters tested at every axis extreme. QA across 40+ browsers and OS versions.

04 / SPECIMEN

The specimen is the contract. Every glyph documented. Every feature demonstrated. Every size tested. Printed and bound. Only then does the typeface exist.
