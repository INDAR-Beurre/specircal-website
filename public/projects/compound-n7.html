§01

Indication / Statement of Purpose

REF · ANNEX A

Compound N-7 is a sterile, shelf-stable nutritive substrate engineered to provide the complete adult human caloric, macronutrient, micronutrient, and electrolytic requirement in a single 410-gram daily dose.

The product is not a meal replacement. It is a meal substitute. Use is intended for environments in which the preparation, acquisition, or social consumption of conventional food is impractical, contraindicated, or operationally prohibited.

Subjects under continuous N-7 administration require no supplementary food, water below daily threshold, or additional micronutrient sources. Long-term sole-source consumption has been observed in clinical cohorts for periods exceeding 1,840 consecutive days without recorded deficiency events.

Active Mechanisms / Functional Components

SUBSECTIONS A – C
03.ASTRUCTURAL

Stabilized Protein Matrix

A heat-stable isolate of soy and pea protein cross-linked under low-shear extrusion. Provides 90 g of complete amino acid coverage per unit with no detectable allergenic fraction above 0.001%. Digestible coefficient ≥ 0.98.

03.BMETABOLIC

Stable Glycemic Carrier

Complex carbohydrate derived from low-gelatinization tapioca and resistant starch fractions. Engineered to elicit a postprandial glucose response below 7.4 mmol/L in healthy adult subjects at the indicated dose.

04.1 Preparation

Tear pouch at indicated notch. Extrude full contents directly into oral cavity or sanitary receptacle. No mixing, heating, or dilution is required or recommended. Once opened, the unit must be consumed within 30 minutes at ambient temperature.

04.2 Adjacent Intake

Concurrent consumption of conventional food is permitted but redundant. Caloric overlap should not exceed 15% of the indicated cohort dose over any 72-hour interval. Caffeine, ethanol, and pharmacological agents are not contraindicated.

§05

Contraindications / Required Notices

MANDATORY · DO NOT REMOVE
! Warning
Tier II Read in full prior to acknowledgment.

The following conditions constitute absolute or relative contraindication. Subjects matching any criterion shall not initiate Compound N-7 administration without prior written authorization from a registered clinician of record.

  1. Subjects below 18 years of chronological age. Pediatric formulation pending review.
  2. Subjects with diagnosed renal insufficiency (eGFR < 45 mL/min/1.73 m²).
  3. Pregnancy, lactation, or planned conception within 90 days of cycle initiation.
  4. Documented hypersensitivity to soy, pea, or encapsulating lipid carriers.
  5. Concurrent administration of MAO inhibitors or anticoagulant regimens above standard prophylactic dose.
  6. Long-term sole-source administration exceeding 1,840 consecutive days without quarterly serum panel review.
