Private notes from the moth-wing dust preserved beneath library lamps, 1912 to 1938

The Lamp Dust Ledger

Each entry is a small remainder of night: powder gathered from porcelain shades, folded into envelopes, and left unnamed until the paper learned the room.

Nothing here was meant to last. That is why it was kept.

A room inventory, copied from a caretaker's blue notebook after the north reading wing was sealed.

Three envelopes, nearly empty

Envelope 04, Birch Room
Dust the color of cooled tea, with one silver filament.

Found under the left lamp, where a grammar of Finnish verbs remained open for nine winters. The page beneath it had browned in the shape of a hand.

Envelope 17, East Alcove
A pale green trace, almost mistaken for the breath of copper.

The label says only: after storm. No date. No collector. The dust still gathers along one corner as though listening for rain inside the roof.

Envelope 29, Closed Stacks
Charcoal, clay, and a faint blue at the folded edge.

Removed from a lamp that no longer had a switch. Nearby, seven unread books carried the same soft print of wings against their spines.
