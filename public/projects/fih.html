01 / Manifesto

We do not repurpose.
We reissue.

A pressure gauge, after 412 days in vacuum, is no longer a measurement instrument. It is a witness. Atelier Gagarine catalogs, certifies and gently re-houses such witnesses for civilian life — without sanding off the scorch marks, and without lying about where they came from.

Every object we sell will have failed at its original job. This is the point.

Nothing in our inventory is decorative in the soft sense. There is a Soyuz 7K re-entry ceramic lamp on the third floor of a Moscow apartment whose scorch pattern will never repeat. It does not need to. Restraint is not a virtue we practice — it is a property of the material. A titanium pressure housing, anodized once, never re-anodized, will keep its color until the heat death of the building.

— From the workshop charter, ratified 14 March 2014, in a bunker in Kzyl-Orda.

House Rules / III
  • I.
    No re-paint. If the polyurethane foam yellowed in orbit, it yellows in your living room too.
  • II.
    No false nostalgia. We are not a souvenir shop. Each piece ships with its decommissioning certificate.
  • III.
    No new fasteners. Where a 1972 rivet survives, we re-use it. We do not pretend.
  • IV.
    No silent upgrades. Every modification is hand-stamped on the chassis in Cyrillic.

04 / Process

Three hands, no sandpaper.

From the moment an object arrives in Kzyl-Orda to the day it leaves in a welded trunk, exactly three people handle it. The restorer who decommissions, the cataloger who records, the cratermaker who packs. We do not have a fourth.

SHP-14
RUS
PHASE 01

Receipt & Disassembly

Every object is disassembled only as far as needed to confirm it is, in fact, what the field team reported. We do not "restore to working order" — we confirm the damage is real, the lineage is honest, and the object is stable enough to live indoors for another century.

OKB-1
1968
PHASE 02

Cataloging & Certificate

Each piece is photographed in raking light, weighed to the gram, and assigned a Cyrillic-only serial. A two-page certificate, on 200gsm cotton paper, is hand-stapled and signed by the restorer. The certificate is the object, legally. The thing in the crate is the evidence.

AG-S
QC
PHASE 03

Re-housing & Departure

If the object is to be functional — a lamp, a clock, a sconce — the re-housing is reversible. Every new fastener is documented on the chassis. The crate is welded aluminium, lined with phenolic foam, and labeled in three languages. The truck driver is also the cataloger.
