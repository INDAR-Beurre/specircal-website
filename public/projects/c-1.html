A listening room for distant weather

A quiet archive of rainfalls, gathered from forests no one is hurrying through.

Each recording is left untouched. The hiss, the dripping, the small interruptions of birds — all of it kept as it was found.

An Introduction

We began by accident. A field recorder left running beside a fallen cedar; an afternoon of rain that no one had asked to remember. When we returned home and listened, the room grew softer around us.

Since then we have travelled, quietly and without much aim, to forests where rain still falls without an audience. We do not edit. We do not arrange. We bring back the recording and shelve it here.
