§ 02 — plate of the month

What we found under the carpet.

Survey №047
Fig. 12 — substrate scan, layer 3 of 7. Subfloor printed with the dye of a discontinued Axminster pattern, c. 1981.
Plate Nº047 / 22Pigment on cotton, 84×118cm

Eleven acres of burnt wool, drawn from below.

For four months we crawled the slab. The depot was levelled in 1998 and rebuilt as a logistics shed; nobody had ever drawn what the fire actually consumed. We did, in seven layers — the carpet, the carpet under the carpet, the kiln-cracked tile, the wartime rebar.

Printed in five passes on a Stanhope flatbed. Folded once. Posted in a cardboard tube nobody will throw away.

Cartographer

R. Asare-Mensah

§ 03

How we work — slowly, & by foot.

Three movements
01
We trespass, politely.
A site is walked between four and forty times. We talk to who is left. We photograph nothing of faces, everything of edges. No drones, no satellites — the sky has already been mapped.
Avg. duration · 9 weeks
02
We draw it three times wrong.
First in pencil, on tracing paper laid over the official record. Then in ink, after the record is set aside. Then a third time — from memory, two days later, in another room. The third drawing is the one that survives.
Avg. duration · 5 weeks
03
We print, sign, & let it go.
Editions are small, numbered, and never reprinted in identical form. Every plate is signed twice — once by the cartographer, once by a person who was there. If we cannot find the second signature, the plate is not released.
Avg. duration · 11 days
