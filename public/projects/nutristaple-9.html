1. Indication

NutriStaple-9™ is indicated for the complete nutritional maintenance of adult humans (≥ 18 years of age) in circumstances where conventional food acquisition, preparation, or consumption is infeasible, inadvisable, or logistically contraindicated. It is formulated to meet or exceed the macronutrient and micronutrient reference intake values established under GNSB Directive 884.12(c), Schedule A, without reliance on any agriculturally derived input stream.

This formulation is not a dietary supplement. It is a total nutrition replacement. When administered as the sole source of caloric intake, NutriStaple-9™ sustains baseline metabolic function for a period not exceeding 540 consecutive days, after which a supervised dietary reintroduction protocol is mandated per GNSB Directive 884.12(c), Annex IV.

This product has not been evaluated for use in pediatric populations, gestating individuals, or persons with diagnosed metabolic disorders of the urea cycle, phenylketonuria, or maple syrup urine disease. Refer to Contraindications (§ 5).

2. Active Mechanisms

NutriStaple-9™ operates through four parallel delivery vectors:

(a) Pre-Hydrolyzed Macronutrient Matrix. Proteins are reduced to constituent di- and tri-peptides via controlled enzymatic cleavage. Long-chain fatty acids are micro-emulsified with a synthetic phospholipid monolayer (DPPC-analogue) at a mean droplet diameter of 0.4 µm. Carbohydrates are supplied as maltodextrin (DE 12) and crystalline D-glucose in a 3:1 mass ratio.

(b) Chelated Micronutrient Payload. All mineral species (Ca, Mg, Fe, Zn, Se, Cu, Mn, Cr, Mo, I) are presented as bis-glycinate chelates or equivalent bioavailable coordination complexes suspended in a pH-buffered aqueous medium (pH 6.8 ± 0.1).

(c) Stabilized Vitamin Ensemble. Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D₂, E, K₁) are microencapsulated in a synthetic amylose shell resistant to oxidation and photodegradation. Water-soluble vitamins (B₁, B₂, B₃, B₅, B₆, B₇, B₉, B₁₂, C) are stabilized as crystalline suspensions in a low-water-activity matrix.

(d) Gastric Transit Modulation. A 0.8% (w/w) addition of synthetic methylcellulose (viscosity grade: 4,000 cP at 2% aqueous) ensures controlled gastric emptying and mitigates osmotic diarrhea at full-dose administration.

5. Dosage / Administration

Standard Adult Protocol (70 kg reference mass): Administer 243 g of NutriStaple-9™ suspension per 24-hour period, divided into three equal aliquots of 81 g each, at intervals not less than 5 hours and not more than 9 hours apart. Each aliquot shall be reconstituted with potable water at a ratio of 1:2.5 (w/w) immediately prior to ingestion. The resulting mixture shall be agitated for no fewer than 30 seconds and consumed within 15 minutes of reconstitution.

Initiation Protocol: For first-time recipients, a 72-hour graduated introduction is required. Day 1: one-third standard aliquot (27 g) administered once. Day 2: two-thirds standard aliquot (54 g) administered twice, 8 hours apart. Day 3: full protocol as described above. Monitoring for gastrointestinal distress is required throughout the initiation window.

Discontinuation: Abrupt cessation after ≥ 14 days of exclusive NutriStaple-9™ consumption is contraindicated. A 7-day graduated reintroduction of conventional foodstuffs is required per GNSB Directive 884.12(c), Annex IV, Protocol R-3.

6. Warnings & Contraindications

Absolute Contraindications: Hereditary fructose intolerance (due to trace sorbitol from the encapsulation matrix); severe hepatic insufficiency (Child-Pugh Class C); untreated hypothyroidism (may reduce basal metabolic rate below the caloric provision threshold of 1,000 kcal/day for a 70 kg reference mass).

Adverse Reactions (Observed in Phase III Trials, n = 4,281): Mild osmotic diarrhea (14.2% of recipients, resolving within 72 hours of continued use); transient cephalalgia (6.7%); oral mucosal desquamation (2.1%, attributed to the mildly acidic pH of reconstituted suspension); urticaria of unknown etiology (0.4%). No serious adverse events were attributed to the formulation in the registration dataset.

⚠ Regulatory Warning
Prolonged exclusive reliance on NutriStaple-9™ (exceeding 540 consecutive days) without supervised dietary reintroduction is associated with a statistically significant reduction in gut microbiome diversity (Shannon index reduction of 34% ± 8%, p < 0.001) and an elevated risk of micronutrient imbalance due to chelation-compound accumulation in adipose tissue. Mandatory monitoring of serum trace element panels at 90-day intervals is required for any administration period exceeding 180 days.
