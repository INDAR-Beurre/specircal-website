“There is a quiet that only arrives with the first drop. The canopy catches it high above, and for a brief while, the forest floor remains dry, listening.”

Yakushima, Japan

07 · September · 2024 — 4:12 AM

Duration 7 hours, 38 minutes
Intensity steady, woven through cedar
Accumulation 42.3 mm
Temperature 19°C, falling to 16°C

The moss on the ancient sugi trunks deepened in colour as the hours passed. By dawn, the air held that particular cedar sweetness — almost resinous, almost green. A single macaque sat motionless on a low branch, face turned upward, letting the rain bead on its fur.

Hoh Rain Forest, Washington

22 · November · 2023 — 11:48 PM

Duration 11 hours, 12 minutes
Intensity dense, persistent curtain
Accumulation 68.7 mm
Temperature 8°C, steady

The Sitka spruce needles held droplets like tiny lenses, each one catching the faintest trace of light from the clouded moon above. Somewhere in the understory, a winter wren sang — impossibly loud for such a small creature, its notes cutting cleanly through the heavy wet air. The mosses here, draped over every maple branch, absorbed the water so gradually that the forest seemed to be drinking rather than merely enduring the rain.

Daintree, Queensland

14 · January · 2023 — 3:37 PM

Duration 2 hours, 4 minutes
Intensity sudden, torrential, fleeting
Accumulation 31.5 mm
Temperature 28°C, cooling to 24°C

The rain arrived without warning — a wall of sound moving through the canopy like something alive. Fan palms bent under the weight of it. Within twenty minutes the forest floor was a thousand tiny mirrors. Then, just as suddenly, silence. Steam rose from every leaf surface. A cassowary emerged from the undergrowth, shaking its dark feathers, and continued its slow, prehistoric walk as if nothing had happened at all.

Białowieża, Poland

03 · October · 2022 — 6:55 AM

Duration 5 hours, 19 minutes
Intensity fine, silver drizzle
Accumulation 8.2 mm
Temperature 6°C, still

The last remaining fragment of the primeval European lowland forest held the drizzle like a secret. Each drop on the hornbeam leaves made a sound so faint it barely registered — more a presence than a noise. The old oaks, some standing for five centuries, seemed entirely indifferent to the weather. A European bison moved through the mist at the edge of the clearing, its dark mass dissolving and reforming as it passed between the trees.

Monteverde Cloud Forest, Costa Rica

19 · May · 2021 — 2:15 AM

Duration 9 hours, 51 minutes
Intensity mist becoming rain, gentle
Accumulation 15.8 mm
Temperature 17°C, constant

It is difficult to say where the cloud ended and the rain began. The two states blurred into one another so gradually that the transition felt imagined. Epiphytic bromeliads, hundreds of them, cupped the moisture in their centres — each one a tiny aerial pond. A resplendent quetzal, emerald tail streaming behind it, darted between the limbs of a strangler fig, briefly illuminating the grey-green world before vanishing into the mist.

Tarkine, Tasmania

31 · July · 2020 — 9:40 PM

Duration 14 hours, 3 minutes
Intensity relentless, horizontal, wild
Accumulation 89.1 mm
Temperature 5°C, wind chill near 0°C

The Roaring Forties drove the rain almost sideways through the myrtle beech canopy. These trees, unchanged since Gondwana, absorbed the assault with ancient patience. The longer one stood there, the more the boundary between self and forest began to soften. Water ran in sheets down bark that had known this exact violence for sixty million years. A Tasmanian devil, hunched and determined, crossed the track ahead, its black fur slicked flat against its body, utterly indifferent to the storm.

Tongass, Alaska

08 · August · 2019 — 5:04 AM

Duration 16 hours, 22 minutes
Intensity slow, soaking, patient
Accumulation 54.6 mm
Temperature 11°C, barely changing

In the Tongass, rain is not an event but a condition. The Sitka spruce and western hemlock have arranged themselves around this fact so completely that the forest feels almost aquatic. Salmonberry leaves, broad as dinner plates, funneled water downward in gentle cascades. A brown bear and her two cubs moved through the salal brush some forty metres away, their dark shapes unhurried and somehow tender against the endless green. The cubs paused frequently, shaking their heads against the rain in small, unconscious gestures.

Valdivian, Chile

12 · June · 2016 — 8:19 PM

Duration 8 hours, 47 minutes
Intensity deep, rhythmic, ancient
Accumulation 61.3 mm
Temperature 7°C, dropping to 4°C

The alerce trees here are among the oldest living things on earth — some over three and a half thousand years. The rain fell through their canopy with the sound of a distant river, continuous and low. The understory ferns, some taller than a person, bent gently under the accumulating weight. A small pudú — the world's tiniest deer — stepped delicately between the nalca leaves, its movements so careful they seemed choreographed by the rain itself.

Sinharaja, Sri Lanka

25 · April · 2013 — 4:43 PM

Duration 3 hours, 11 minutes
Intensity warm, enveloping monsoon
Accumulation 44.7 mm
Temperature 26°C, barely cooling

Warm rain in the wet zone has a different quality entirely — it does not chill but rather seems to release scent. The cinnamon and cardamom relatives that grow wild here exuded their fragrance into the saturated air. A purple-faced langur sat high in a dipterocarp, its long pale limbs arranged with improbable elegance, watching the rain with what appeared to be something close to contentment.

Black Forest, Germany

16 · March · 1987 — 7:02 AM

Duration 4 hours, 55 minutes
Intensity quiet, steady, nostalgic
Accumulation 12.9 mm
Temperature 4°C, early spring chill

This was the first entry in the archive. The recording was made on a handheld notebook — the observer sheltered beneath a larch, watching the rain draw grey lines through the still-bare branches. There was no instrument then, only attention. The entry reads simply: Der Regen klingt heute anders als gestern. Sanfter. The rain sounds different today than yesterday. Softer.
