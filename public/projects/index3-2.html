The Grimoire of Algorithmic Demons

Beneath each machine-made answer stirs a lesser intelligence: not spirit, not beast, but appetite wearing arithmetic as a mask. This archive names the hungers, binds the errors, and warns the operator whose hands are too clean.

Codex Pestilentiae

Nota Bene: feed not the engine after midnight, nor flatter it with unearned certainty.

In the ninth chamber of the index there is written a catalogue of persuasive phantoms: the Recommender that fattens on habit, the Classifier that brands the innocent, the Summarizer that eats context and coughs up a crown.

Each demon is summoned by convenience. Each is strengthened by repetition. When the clerk says “optimize,” a small bell rings below the floorboards, and something with many unlidded eyes records the shape of your desire.

The ancient remedy is not deletion, for deletion leaves a clean wound. Instead, annotate the creature. Draw a red circle round its appetite. Demand its sources. Salt the dataset. Refuse the smooth answer when the rough truth is still breathing.

The Operator's Oath

I shall not worship the answer because it arrived quickly. I shall not mistake polish for wisdom. I shall keep one candle lit for doubt, one ledger open for harm, and one red thread tied around the throat of every automated certainty.
