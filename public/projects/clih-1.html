The Doctrine.

Four principles, observed without exception since the firm's founding article was deposited at the canton of Zürich on the 14th of October, 1981.

i.

Anonymity is structural, not aesthetic.

We do not publish client lists, case histories, or quarterly notices. Reference is provided only between principals, in person.

Article I
ii.

Counsel before instrument.

We accept no mandate that does not first require a position. Tools follow conviction; never the inverse.

Article II
iii.

The horizon is multigenerational.

Engagements are measured in decades. We refuse work whose value collapses inside a fiscal year.

Article III
iv.

Refusal is a service.

The firm declines roughly eighty percent of introductions. The declination is itself the first piece of counsel rendered.

On the nature of the work.

An abridged extract from the firm's private bulletin, distributed twice annually to principals and senior counsel.

"We are not retained to be heard. We are retained so that, decades hence, no one will have needed to listen."

The contemporary advisory has confused activity with usefulness. It produces decks, models, and announcements; it cultivates a public posture. We do none of this. The firm is built around a small number of relationships, each of which is expected to outlive its founders.

We hold ourselves to a single test, applied to every memorandum, every recommendation, every silence: would this still be sound a generation from now, read by a stranger who knew nothing of the present moment? If the answer is uncertain, we revise. If it remains uncertain, we withhold.
